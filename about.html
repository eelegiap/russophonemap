About the Project

Historical Context

In this map, I am interested in mapping Russian language use over the former republics of the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics (1922-1991). The former Soviet republics include Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania, Belarus, Russia, Ukraine, Moldova, Armenia, Georgia, Kazakhstan, Turkmenistan, Tajikistan, Uzbekistan, and Azerbaijan. Although Russian was not an "official state language", it served as the language of interethnic communication (Russian: язык межнационального общения) in the USSR.
